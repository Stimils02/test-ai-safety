Our Community

The UMass Amherst AI Safety Initiative brings together a diverse group of researchers, faculty, students, and staff committed to ensuring that artificial intelligence systems are developed and deployed safely, ethically, and for the benefit of humanity.

Leadership

Dr. Sarah Johnson

Director

Dr. Johnson is a Professor of Computer Science specializing in machine learning interpretability and AI alignment. She previously led research teams at DeepMind and has published extensively on AI safety.

Our Funding

The UMass Amherst AI Safety Initiative is supported by a diverse range of funding sources, including federal grants, industry partnerships, and philanthropic foundations. We are grateful for their support in advancing our mission.

Major Grants

National Science Foundation

Robust and Interpretable AI Systems

$3.5 Million | 2022-2025

This grant supports research on developing interpretable AI systems that maintain robust performance across diverse and challenging environments.

Department of Energy

AI Safety for Critical Infrastructure

$2.8 Million | 2023-2026

This project focuses on ensuring the safety and security of AI systems deployed in critical energy infrastructure applications.

DARPA

Assured Autonomy Program

$1.9 Million | 2021-2024

This grant supports research on formal verification methods for autonomous AI systems to ensure they operate safely within specified parameters.

Advisory Board

Our Advisory Board brings together distinguished experts from academia, industry, and policy to provide strategic guidance for the AI Safety Initiative. Board members help shape our research agenda, educational programs, and outreach activities.

Dr. Elizabeth Chen

Professor of Computer Science, Stanford University

Dr. Chen is a leading researcher in AI ethics and governance. Her work focuses on developing frameworks for responsible AI innovation.

Dr. James Wilson

Chief AI Safety Officer, Tech Company

Dr. Wilson brings industry perspective on implementing AI safety measures in commercial applications and large-scale deployments.

Dr. Maria Rodriguez

Director, AI Policy Institute

Dr. Rodriguez specializes in AI policy and regulation, working at the intersection of technology, law, and public policy.

Dr. David Park

Professor of Philosophy, MIT

Dr. Park explores philosophical questions related to AI alignment, machine ethics, and the long-term implications of advanced AI systems.

Dr. Sarah Johnson

Former Chief Scientist, Government Agency

Dr. Johnson brings expertise in AI safety from a national security perspective, focusing on robust and secure AI systems.

Dr. Robert Kim

Director, National AI Research Institute

Dr. Kim has led major national initiatives in AI research and development. He specializes in coordinating multi-stakeholder efforts to address AI safety challenges.
